I'll Be Missing You

Puff Daddy and Faith Evans feat. 112

Quick Summary

"I'll Be Missing You" is a song by Puff Daddy (aka P Diddy) and Faith Evans featuring R&B group 112 in memory of Christopher "The Notorious B.I.G" Wallace. It was released as a second single from the album No Way Out, and samples 1983 hit song "Every Breath You Take" by The Police. The song won Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group at the 40th Grammy Awards. The song spent eleven weeks at the top of the US Billboard Hot 100 and reached number one in a few other countries, This has become one of the bets-selling singles of all time.

Song Composition

"I'll Be Missing You" samples "Every Breath You Take", a 1983 single by The Police, and uses an interpolation of the melody which is sung by Fatih Evans. Rapper Sauce Money composed the verses, and a melody for a hymn "I'll Fly Away" was also reused in the track. Police songwriter sing did not give permission for the use of the sample and sued, receiving 100% of the song royalties. Although this song was performed at the 1997 MTV Video Music Awards by Sting alongside Puff Daddy and Faith Evans.
